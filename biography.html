Biography

Sadra Sadraddini is a Staff AI Engineer at Sony AI. Previously he was a Principal Robotics Engineer at Dexai Robotics and a Postdoctoral Associate at MIT CSAIL in the Robot Locomotion Group led by Professor Russ Tedrake.

He received his Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Boston University in January 2018, where he was a member of the Hybrid and Networked Systems (HyNeSs) Group in the BU Robotics Lab, advised by Professor Calin Belta. His doctoral dissertation is titled "Formal Methods for Resilient Control".

Prior to his doctoral studies, Sadra received dual B.Sc. degrees in Mechanical Engineering and Aerospace Engineering from Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran, in 2013. He was born in 1989 in Tabriz, Iran, and joined Boston University's graduate program in September 2013.

His research sits at the intersection of formal methods, control theory, and robotics. He develops provably correct and safe controllers for complex dynamical systems, including hybrid, nonlinear, and stochastic systems. His work spans optimization-based synthesis, temporal logic specifications, polytope containment, motion planning, and probabilistic verification.
